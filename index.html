Careful writing for emails where consequences matter

I help people draft, rewrite, and de-risk emails and short communications where tone, precision, and institutional dynamics influence interpretation and response.

Typical contexts

  • Emails that create a formal record (complaints, rebuttals, follow-ups)
  • Requests or negotiations where tone affects leverage and response time
  • HR or administrative exchanges involving performance, disputes, or exits
  • Conflict-resolution messages where clarity prevents escalation or liability

In short: situations where a single email can close a door, start a process, or escalate a conflict. Each case is different; effective emails depend on context, constraints, and sequencing—not templates.

How it works

  1. You explain the situation. You email me the context, who the message is for, what you want to achieve, and any constraints or deadlines. A draft is optional.
  2. I confirm the scope. Based on your description, I confirm the appropriate tier and timeline. If something needs adjustment, I say so before starting.
  3. I do the work. Depending on the tier, this may involve advice only, drafting the message, or preparing follow-ups.
  4. You review the result. You receive the advice or draft, along with any included explanation. One revision round is included where applicable.
  5. We continue if needed. If the exchange evolves, continuation support is available for the same situation.

Most work is handled asynchronously by email.

What you get

  • A version that preserves your intent while reducing ambiguity and unnecessary escalation
  • Optional notes on why key changes were made (so you can reuse the pattern)
  • Clear asks, clean structure, and “reply-safe” phrasing
